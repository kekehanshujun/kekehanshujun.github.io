Interpretation

The visible funded set is highly concentrated, but the concentration is mostly explained by the documented founder reserves. Known founder wallets account for 298,179.050744 RTC out of the 299,096.810642 RTC public subset. Removing those founder wallets leaves 917.759898 RTC in publicly resolved community balances, and the leading visible community wallet, `nox-ventures`, holds 817.5 RTC.

This shape is normal for an early small-cap network before broad market distribution: treasury, development, bounty, and liquidity wallets dominate the chart, while active community balances are small and lumpy. Compared with mature large-cap assets, the top-holder concentration is high. Compared with many newly launched small-cap tokens, the public founder labeling is a useful transparency advantage: the largest buckets are named operational reserves rather than unexplained anonymous holders.

The main risk is not the visible Gini coefficient by itself; it is the gap between the public row-by-row data and the node aggregate. A fuller analysis would need a public, paginated, non-sensitive balance export or a signed snapshot file so researchers can reproduce the whole 437,075.354584 RTC balance total without admin credentials.
